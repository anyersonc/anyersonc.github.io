Worked example#

Now go to NanoHub MOSFet to access the simulation tool.

For accessing, recall the \(pn\) junctions worked example for entering into Nanohub simulations.

The objective of this activity is to find the effect on the \(I_\mathrm{D}-V_\mathrm{D}\) curve characteristic caused by the temperature. Follow the next steps to get the results.

Input Parameters#

In this simulation we only are going to modify the temperature with the standard value to observe the isolated effect of the temperature on the device behavior.

\(T_1= 200 \mathrm{K}\)

\(T_2= 400 \mathrm{K}\)

Step by Step procedure#

(a) Enter to Nanohub#

Enter into NanoHub and clic on “Launch tool.”

(b) Find the temperature parameter#

In the tool, clic on the “Model” tab in the part above. It is the middle tab in the tool.

(c) Check the current temperature and change it#

Now in the Model tab, we find the Temperature option. You should clic on that for accessing the temperature change. Note that the temperature is in Kelvin, \(\mathrm{K}\), units.

(d) Choose the first temperature \(T_1\)#

Select the option or write the option of the temperature for the simulation, remember that for this case \(T_1=200\mathrm{K}\)

(e) Simulate the first temperature \(T_1\)#

Once selected, clic on simulate for obtaining the curves.

(f) Check if the simulation produces coherent curves#

The simulation must provide two curves, one red and one blue, that must represent the behavior expected of a MOSFET with a gate voltage \(V_\mathrm{G} \gt V_\mathrm{T}\). In the red curve we will find the case of \(V_\mathrm{D} \lt V_\mathrm{D_\mathrm{sat}}\) and in the blue curve we will find the case for \(V_\mathrm{D} \gt V_\mathrm{D_\mathrm{sat}}\).

(g) Choose the second temperature \(T_2\)#

For the second case, we will simulate in the same simulation, we will not terminate or restart the tool. Now, choosing for the second temperature \(T_2=400\mathrm{K}\).

(h) Check if the simulation produces coherent curves#

The simulation must provide two curves, one red and one blue, that must represent the behavior expected of a MOSFET with a gate voltage \(V_\mathrm{G} \gt V_\mathrm{T}\). In the red curve we will find the case of \(V_\mathrm{D} \lt V_\mathrm{D_\mathrm{sat}}\) and in the blue curve we will find the case for \(V_\mathrm{D} \gt V_\mathrm{D_\mathrm{sat}}\).

(i) Overlap the curves for comparison and analysis#

Now with the option in the right inferior side, clic on “All” for plotting the two results at the same time. Now it is posible to compare the two cases and produce an analysis about the effect of the temperature on the behavior of a MOSFET.

Effect of the temperature on the MOSFET behavior#
